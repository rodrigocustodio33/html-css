Weekend Recap: WAG Combs La Ville International Tournament

The American women’s delegation won nine medals over the weekend, including gold for Brooklyn Klauser (Frisco, Texas/WOGA Gymnastics), at the 2025 Combs La Ville International Tournament in France. Klauser won the Hopes floor exercise title with a 12.866 and took bronze on vault (12.750) as one of three delegation members to win a pair of apparatus medals at the competition. Avery Haines (Bowie, Md./First State Gymnastics) earned silver on uneven bars with a 12.433 and bronze on balance beam with a 11.400, and Kaylee Sath (Lee’s Summit, Mo./Great American Gymnastics Express) won bronze on bars (12.366) and vault (12.916) in the junior division. Junior Kylie Smith (Loveland, Ohio/Cincinnati Gymnastics) also won an apparatus honor, taking floor bronze with a 12.500. In all-around competition, Klauser was fourth (49.200) and Audrey VanGrinsven (Champlin, Minn./Pacific Reign Gymnastics) fifth (49.150) in Hopes, while Smith was fifth (50.133) and Sath sixth (49.699) among juniors. Both American teams secured silver medals in team competition Saturday. The junior team of Sath, Smith and Trinity Wood (Clinton, Md./Capital Gymnastics National Training Center) won silver (100.698) between teams from host-country France who placed first (107.530) and third (99.732). The Hopes team of Haines, Klauser and VanGrinsven won silver (99.300) behind France in first (101.650) and ahead of Canada in third (95.250).

Brooke Bazan

Brooke Bazan got her start in gymnastics at the age of three at a "My Gym." When she turned four, Brooke began taking recreational classes at GMS Gymnastics. A few months after taking recreational classes, Brooke was invited to join the "shooting stars" program and pre-team at age five. After participating in several mini-meets, Brooke competed for the first time as an Xcel Bronze at the age of six. Brooke competed two meets of Xcel Bronze where she earned an all around score of above 38.0 at both meets. Brooked competed one meet of level 3 in 2019 before moving to Capital Gymnastics. During the spring 2019 season, Brooke competed level 4 and went on to finish third on vault and beam at the Virginia State Championships. As a level 5 during the 2019-2020 season, Brooke began her season at Christmas on the Chesapeake where she won the beam title and came in second on the all around. At her next two meets, Brooke finished second and first in the all around, respectively. Unfortunately, due to the COVID-19 pandemic, Brooke's season was cut short as the remainder of the season was cancelled. In 2021 Brooke was able to compete at the Virginia State Championships where she earned a gold medal on floor & silver medals on vault, bars, beam and all around. Brooke also has a spot on 2021's America's Top 100 for floor and all around. Brooke kicked off her 2022 season as a level 8 at the Christmas on the Chesapeake where she earned silver medals on bars and floor. She then competed at Hill's Maryland Classic winning vault, silver in the all around and bronze on bars. Currently, Brooke trains 23 hours per week under coaches Morgan and Tish at Capital Gymnastics. Brooke's goal for her 2021 season is to improve on vault and begin flipping her vault. In the future, Brooke hopes to earn a college scholarship for gymnastics and compete in a U.S. Classic.
